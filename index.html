Airport Orientation

Introduction

Istanbul Ataturk Airport (IATA: IST; ICAO: LTBA) is the largest and busiest airport in Turkey. Passenger traffic at IST grew by over 20% from 2011 to 2012, making it the fastest growing of Europe's 50 busiest airports during that period. In 2012, it was the 20th busiest passenger airport in the world. As of May 2013, direct flights from 64 airlines linked it to at least 220 destinations.

IST 2012 Statistics Summary

  • Passengers: 45,091,962
  • International: 29,812,307
  • International: 29,812,307
  • Domestic: 15,297,655
  • Cargo handled (metric tons): 1.23 million
  • Total Air Movements: 364,322
Location
  • IST is the larger and more-centrally located of the two international airports serving Istanbul. The second airport, Sabiha Gokcen (SHA) is on the eastern side of the Bosphorus.
  • In May 2013, the Turkish government awarded a contract to Limak Holding A.S. (the operators for Sabiha Gokcen Airport) for the development of a new airport on the Black Sea that will replace IST as Istanbul's major international air hub.
  • Of note, IST/s commercial operator, TAV Airports Holding, lost the bid.
Major Features

Overview:
IST facilities are generally clustered in groups supporting common operational missions. These include:

  • Passenger Ops (International and Domestic)
  • Cargo Ops (International and Domestic)
  • Flight Training
  • Turkish Air Force

Runway Configuration:
IST has three runways. The two longer runways (17R/35L & 17L/35R) run parallel in a north-south direction, while the third (05/23) runs northeast-southwest. Runway 05/23 is not long enough to handle some of the larger aircraft operating at IST.

Security

Introduction

Passenger screening processes at Istanbul Ataturk Airport (IST) are thorough and exceed ICAO requirements.

General Findings:

  • Multiple layers of effective security screening
  • Effectively using metal detectors and x-ray machines
  • Robust investment in wide range of technologies
  • Good balance of females thoroughly checking niqab (veils)
